Seattle Center

Seattle Center consists of the Space Needle, the Museum of Pop Culture, Chihuly Garden and Glass, Pacific Science Center, and more. In our opinion, the Space Needle is not worth the money. The view just ok. However, it was recently renovated to have a glass bottom, which may add some excitement. (For a better view, see Sky View Observatory). The Chihuly Garden and Glass is amazing, but expensive, and MoPoP is an all-day adventure with some fun interactive music exhibits.

Sky View Observatory

This is the better version of the Space Needle experience. There's no glass bottom, but the observatory is on top of Columbia Tower, the tallest building in the state at 76 floors and 933 ft. You can see everything in the area, including across the water to Bellevue, and in the summer there's a good chance of spotting Mt. Rainier! There's a restaurant and bar in the observatory as well.

Pike Place Market and Gum Wall

A classic favorite for both tourists and locals! Pike Place Market is full of food, art, and souvenirs. This is where you'll find the guys that throw fish that you've probably seen on the Food Network. You can also find the first Starbucks located here. You'll know you've found it when you find the multi-block line. Make sure to check out the Gum Wall, located down the stairs right next to the fish throwers, and leave your mark!

Snoqualmie Falls

Snoqualmie Falls is a great way to see some of Seattle's beautiful landscape without the hike. You can park at either the upper or the lower viewing area, and can hike the paved 1.3 mile trail between the two. Fans of Twin Peaks will recognize the Salish Lodge at the top of the falls as The Great Northern Hotel.

Gas Works Park

Gas Works is a park at the site of an old coal gasification plant. It's a great place for a picnic with a view of the Lake Union and the Space Needle directly opposite.

Seattle Aquarium

We haven't been here ourselves, but we've heard great things about it!

Starbucks Reserve Roastery

Coffee lovers should visit the first Starbucks Reserve Roastery in Capitol Hill! Try out the always changing experimental menu items or do a guided tasting flight.

Golden Gardens Park

Golden Gardens Park offers hiking, fishing, and a beach facing out into the sound. Enjoy built-in bonfires at night, but be sure to get there early to claim your spot! Please remember that this is a Seattle beach, so even in the peak of the summer, the water is very cold.

Magnuson Park

Magnuson Park is a 350-acre park located on the spot of the former Naval Station Puget Sound on Lake Washington. This is a good spot to swim or float. The water will be chilly, but is usually bearable by late summer.

UW Waterfront Activities Center

WAC is located next to Husky Stadium on Union Bay. You can rent canoes, rowboats, and kayaks for $12-$20/hour. The bay is well protected, so the waters are smooth, so it's a great place inexperienced rowers and kayakers.

Take a Ferry to Bainbridge Island

Bainbridge is an island right across Elliot Bay from downtown Seattle. Spend a day visiting the shops and restaurants or taking a wine tasting tour. You can get to Bainbridge by taking a 30 minute ferry from downtown Seattle.

Float the Snoqualmie River

By late July/early August, the river is at the perfect balance of temperature and water level for floating! The river is pretty tame overall, but please pay attention to signage. There is one area that can be dangerous if you don't pay attention to the signage and get out at the appropriate time. If you pay attention, you won't have any issues. You can rent tubes and take the shuttle through Fall City Floating.

Mount Rainier National Park

Mount Rainier is an active volcano located 59 miles south-southeast of Seattle, in Mount Rainier National Park.

Seattle Underground Tour

The Seattle we know now is actually built on top of the original Seattle, which burned down in 1889. The underground tours take you beneath the streets of Seattle to see what's left of the original structures. There are two tour companies: Bill Speidel's Underground Tour and Beneath the Streets. We've done the Underground Tour several times, and will always go back for more. We genereally prefer Bill Speidel's, but both are very good. Beneath the Streets also offers an 18+ Red Light District tour, and Bill Spidel's offers a paranormal version of the tour.
