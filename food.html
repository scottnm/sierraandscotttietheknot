Breakfast

Top Pot Doughnuts

A local Seattle favorite! Top Pot is the doughnut store of Seattle. You may notice the wide selection of cake doughnuts as opposed to the lighter, fluffier raised doughnuts you’re probably more used to. Cake doughnuts are the thing around here. Top Pot has locations all over Seattle.

Patty's, Pete's, and Voula's

These are actually 3 different restaurants: Patty’s Egg Nest, Pete’s Egg Nest and Voula’s Offshore Cafe. Why are they lumped together? Well, Patty opened her restaurant first, then sold a franchise to Pete and Voula, who later renamed it Pete’s Egg Nest. Voula’s aunt, also named Voula, opened Voula’s Offshore Cafe. They’re all delicious breakfast restaurants with similar offerings, including Mediterranean inspired dishes (makes sense since all of the original owners were also Greek).

Lunch/Dinner

Seattle is full of great food, but here are a few of our favorites!

Ivar's Fish Bar

Ivar's Fish Bar is the quintessential local Seattle restaurant. Visit the waterfront location for a nice view. See the menu here. (Make sure you try the clam chowder -- It’s the specialty!)

Ivar's Acres of Clams

Want a fancier atmosphere? Ivar’s Acres of Clams is attached to the Fish Bar, but it’s a much fancier sit-down restaurant. You can still get the classic clam chowder, or for the non-seafood eaters in your group, the steak and chicken options are just as delicious. This is one of our favorite places to take visitors!

Din Tai Fung and Dough Zone

Din Tai Fung and Dough Zone are fantastic dim sum restaurants. Dim sum is a type of Chinese cuisine that consists of small bite-sized portions of food, usually served family-style. Both of these restaurants are another favorite for our out-of-town guests. Both restaurants have locations near the Downtown/Westlake light rail stops in Downtown Seattle. One important note: these are very popular restaurants, so the wait is often long. Plan ahead of time and get in line before you’re absolutely starving. You may be able to check-in through Yelp to secure your place in line before you arrive.
Need recommendations for what to get? Our usual order is pork Xiao Long Bao or “soup dumplings” and steamed pork buns (sometimes called Kurobuta buns). 1 order of each usually feeds the two of us, but it’s always easy to order more!

Delfino's Pizza

Delfino's has delicious Chicago Style pizza, but make sure you are prepared to wait! It takes about 30 minutes for deep dish and stuffed pizzas, but it’s worth it!

Ramen

Seattle has a lot of really good ramen restaurants. If you’ve only ever had at-home packaged ramen, then you have try the real thing while you’re in town! Kizuki Ramen & Izakaya and Arashi Ramen are two popular restaurants in the area.

Piroshki on 3rd

You may recognize this one from Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives ! Piroshki on 3rd has been Sierra’s go-to lunch restaurant ever since her first internship back in 2015. Don’t know what a Piroshki is? Think empanadas! A warm bready casing filled with savory meats, veggies, and seasonings. There’s another popular piroshki restaurant in town called Piroshky Piroshky you may want to try, but Sierra much prefers Piroshki on 3rd!

Pel Meni Dumpling Tzar

While on the topic of delicious Russian foods, we also highly recommend the Dumpling Tzar. Similar to Asian dumplings, Pel Meni are bite-sized dumplings filled with warm meats and potatoes. The Capitol Hill location is very small and has limited seating, but the Fremont location has more space.

Beecher's Handmade Cheese

Beecher’s is another classic local Seattle restaurant (really, we’re full of them). Stop by during your trip to Pike Place Market for a bowl of Mac & Cheese (Sierra recommends the Mariachi Mac & Cheese!) and watch the cheesemakers at work! If you don’t get a chance to try it while you’re here, no worries! There’s a location in the airport as well!

Ezell's Famous Chicken

Missing that Southern fried chicken already? So do we. Ezell’s is the only restaurant in Seattle that makes fried chicken the right way. That’s because the family moved from Texas to Seattle and ran into the same issue: Seattleites don’t know good fried chicken! Get regular or spicy chicken with all the fixins!

Dessert & Specialty

Salt & Straw

And here we begin the great debate… Salt & Straw or Molly Moons? Don’t let the smell fresh waffle cones fool you! While Molly Moon’s may be the local Seattle ice cream shop, Salt & Straw is the far superior choice (at least in our opinion). Don’t be scared off by the menu (or the line). The flavors may look strange, but I guarantee you they’re all amazing. Salt & Straw is designed for you to have the freedom to taste every single flavor on the menu, so please do! You never know what kind of bizarre sounding flavor you might actually end up loving. Salt & Straw has both vegan and regular ice cream, and I promise you cannot tell the difference. They are located in both Capitol Hill or Ballard.

Molly Moon's Homemade Ice Cream

Ok, Molly Moon’s is actually pretty good, and the fresh waffle cones are amazing. They do offer some out of the ordinary, very Seattle, flavors, though not quite as unique as those at S&S. If you go to Molly Moon’s, absolutely get the waffle cone. It’s their specialty. They have locations all over, including Capitol Hill and University Village near UW.

Cinnaholic

Cinnaholic is a gourmet cinnamon roll bakery where you can top your fresh cinnamon roll with any number of crazy toppings. It’s as amazing as it sounds.

Food Trucks

Seattle is covered in Food Trucks. You can see a list of food trucks and their scheduled locations here. Sierra recommends Alaskan Dumplings (pel meni again!) and BeanFish (taiyaki — Japanese fish-shaped waffles stuffed with sweet and savory fillings).

Friday Afternoon Tea

For tea drinkers like Sierra, Friday Afternoon Tea is a great experience! The store is owned and operated by Friday Elliott, who has Lexical-Gustatory Synesthesia, which allows her to taste words and ideas. She creates all kinds of interesting blends for every flavor profile. You can order a hot cup of tea or buy loose leaf tea for later. Sierra particularly enjoys perusing the fandom blends for Sailor Moon, Harry Potter, and a lot more. 
For an extra-fun experience, you can schedule a custom blending appointment with Friday! Bring her a concept, and she’ll blend a tea for you right then and there! You can bring her a song, an idea, a person… almost anything you want can be turned into a custom blend! Make sure to schedule ahead of time if you want to do an in-person appointment. You can read more about how Friday creates these blends here.

Drinks

You can find bars all over the place here, especially in Capitol Hill, but these are some of the favorites.

Navy Strength

As many of you know, Sierra isn’t a big drinker, but if she had to choose a favorite bar, it would be this one. Navy Strength is a tiki bar with all the best, sweetest, most tropical drinks, and the food is good too! There are also a few mocktails available (Grace O’Malley is Sierra’s go-to). It’s located in Belltown, near Downtown Seattle.

Rumba

If you like rum, this is your place. Rumba has a massive menu of rums from all over the world, and their selection changes often based on what they’re able to acquire. For food, Sierra recommends the empanadas and the pork rinds.

Optimism Brewing

Located in Capitol Hill, Optimism is a local brewery that is both kid and dog-friendly. You can check the website to see which food truck they have out back during your visit and see upcoming events, including the many trivia nights they host.

Fremont Brewing Company

Fremont Brewing is another iconic brewery in the area with nice indoor and outdoor seating and free boardgames to play while you drink.

Tavern Law and Needle & Thread

We’ve never been here, but Tavern Law is a popular bar and restaurant. Needle & Thread is a reservation-only speakeasy that has a secret entrance inside Tavern Law. There’s no menu -- you just tell them what you like and they will provide you with a few options.

Merchant's Cafe and Saloon, J&M Cafe, and Central Saloon

These 3 bars have a couple things in common: All 3 claim to be the oldest bar in Seattle, and all 3 are considered to be haunted. If you’re looking for the most exciting ghost story and the spookiest atmosphere, Merchant’s Cafe is your place. It’s the only one that readily boasts its haunted status, and most of the bartenders are happy to tell you about the history of the place and their own experiences. There’s also a snug and spooky downstairs bar with some… interesting decor. Meanwhile, Central Saloon has been a key venue for up-and-coming artists, including SoundGarden, Nirvana, and Alice in Chains. And then there's J&M Cafe
